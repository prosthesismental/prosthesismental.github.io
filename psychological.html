Key Psychological Challenges

Grief, Loss & Identity

Amputees often experience a grieving process similar to bereavement, grappling with phantom limb sensations, sadness, and identity disruption. Professional counseling helps individuals navigate these losses.

Body Image & Self‑Esteem

Negative body image correlates with reduced prosthesis compliance. In one study, structured interventions improved acceptance by 35 % among transfemoral amputees (Gozaydinoglu et al. 2019).

Social & Emotional Isolation

Public stigma can deter social participation, intensifying loneliness. Community engagement mitigates these effects and accelerates adaptation.
