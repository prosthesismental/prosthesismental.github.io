Conclusion

Overall, this research highlights how prosthesis adaptation significantly influences amputees' mental health and psychological well-being. Key challenges include grief, identity disruption, body image concerns, and emotional isolation. Comprehensive psychological support, rehabilitation programs, and community engagement substantially mitigate these issues. Technological advancements provide both psychological benefits and new adaptation challenges, emphasizing the need for integrated approaches combining mental health support with prosthetic innovation.

Understanding the psychological dimensions of prosthetic adaptation is fundamental to improving amputees' quality of life. Effective psychological support strategies and technological advancements significantly enhance mental health outcomes, emphasizing the necessity of integrated rehabilitation approaches. Addressing cultural and socioeconomic disparities remains essential for equitable access to prosthetic technologies. Ultimately, holistic rehabilitation programs combining advanced prosthetic technology with comprehensive psychological support represent the future of amputee care, promoting improved psychological resilience, independence, and overall well-being.
