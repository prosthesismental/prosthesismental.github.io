Prosthesis Use & Physical Function

Prosthetic devices restore mobility and independence following limb loss. Upper‑limb options range from passive cosmetic hands to myoelectric and hybrid systems that translate muscle signals into precise hand movements. Lower‑limb devices—such as transtibial and transfemoral prostheses—redistribute weight and replicate gait cycles. Microprocessor‑controlled knees and ankles adapt in real time to speed and terrain, dramatically improving stability.

Mental Health in Amputees

Amputation triggers emotional responses including grief, denial, anxiety, and depression (Murray 2009; Matos et al. 2020). Early psychological support normalizes these reactions and fosters coping strategies, leading to better long‑term adjustment.
